About Us

India is facing an unprecedented humanitarian crisis in the form of covid calamity. The healthcare system is teetering on the verge of a total collapse. Responsible citizens are taking up the challenge of saving the covid stricken, as the hospitals and healthcare infrastructure is extraordinarily burdened. Deaths and mourning have become a daily reality. The crisis is unparalleled because the world's second largest population, developing country infrastructure, and scarcity of critical resources like hospital beds, oxygen manufacturing facilities and unpreparedness on the part of government have left large swathes of people helpless, and in danger of losing lives to fend for themselves, or at the mercy of compassionate citizen volunteers. We have risen to the call of duty to protect our fellow citizens. We are organising a drive to procure supplies of oxygen, oxygen concentrators, medical supplies, and grassroots reach to the nooks and corners of India. We are a network of doctors, Wharton and IIT alumni, coming together to rescue India from this devastation to whatever extent we best can. We solicit your support in donations. We will account for every donation, every rupee spent, and record the recipient details. Together in India Covid Rescue Mission, we will attempt the impossible. Let us save lives. TIME IS OF THE ESSENCE. So forgive our sloppiness. As we focus on prioritising saving of lives.

What to do to protect yourself from Covid-19?

  • Wear a mask that covers your nose and mouth to help protect yourself and others.
    • Everyone 2 years and older should wear masks in public.
    • Masks should be worn in addition to staying at least 6 feet apart, especially around people who don’t live with you.
    • If someone in your household is infected, people in the household should take precautions including wearing masks to avoid spread to others.
    • Wash your hands or use hand sanitizer before putting on your mask.
    • Wear your mask over your nose and mouth and secure it under your chin.
    • Fit the mask snugly against the sides of your face, slipping the loops over your ears or tying the strings behind your head.
    • If you have to continually adjust your mask, it doesn’t fit properly, and you might need to find a different mask type or brand.
    • Make sure you can breathe easily.
  • Stay 6 feet apart from others who don’t live with you.
    • Inside your home: Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
      • If possible, maintain 6 feet between the person who is sick and other household members.
    • Outside your home: Put 6 feet of distance between yourself and people who don’t live in your household.
      • Remember that some people without symptoms may be able to spread virus. Stay at least 6 feet (about 2 arm lengths) from other people. Keeping distance from others is especially important for people who are at higher risk of getting very sick.
  • Get a COVID-19 vaccine when it is available to you.
    • Authorized COVID-19 vaccines can help protect you from COVID-19.
    • You should get a COVID-19 vaccine when it is available to you.
    • Once you are fully vaccinated, you may be able to start doing some things that you had stopped doing because of the pandemic.
  • Avoid crowds and poorly ventilated indoor spaces.
    • Being in crowds like in restaurants, bars, fitness centers, or movie theaters puts you at higher risk for COVID-19.
    • Avoid indoor spaces that do not offer fresh air from the outdoors as much as possible.
    • If indoors, bring in fresh air by opening windows and doors, if possible.
  • Wash your hands often with soap and water. Use hand sanitizer if soap and water aren’t available.
    • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds especially after you have been in a public place, or after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing.
    • It’s especially important to wash:
      • Before eating or preparing food
      • Before touching your face
      • After using the restroom
      • After leaving a public place
      • After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing
      • After handling your mask
      • After changing a diaper
      • After caring for someone sick
      • After touching animals or pets
    • If soap and water are not readily available, use a hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol. Cover all surfaces of your hands and rub them together until they feel dry.
    • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.

  • What to do when you contract covid, or suspect an infection?

    Lay on your stomach with two pillows under your hip and one under your upper chest. Lie on your sides too. This expands the surface area available for respiration. Start monitoring your oxygen saturation. It’s very important to keep doing so even after the fever has dissipated for at least a week. If ct value is over 24 you will not be infectious to others but still take every precaution.

  • Medicines
    • Tab Favipiravir (Favmac) 200 mg Day 1 : 9 tabs twice a day Day 2 onwards till day 7: 4 tabs twice a day ( clinical data shows weak but some efficacy. Since there are minimal side effects it’s worth including in regimen.)
    • Tab Ivermectin 12 mg At bedtime x 3 days
    • Most doctors prescribe a antibiotic like doxy cycline (100 mg twice a day for five days or Azithromycin ( 500 mg once a day for five days ) but quite frankly if you have two freshly chopped pods of raw garlic and you don’t have any other secondary issue you shouldn’t need it.
    • Inhaled Budesonide ( an asthma medicine ) reduces risk that the disease will become more serious.
    • Eat any zinc supplement Zinconia. Is a common and easily available zinc supplement Have one a day for two weeks. My personal preference is to eat a few cashews daily instead.
    • Have paracetamol if fever goes to 102/103 if it’s lower don’t interfere with your fever. Interferon production and efficacy increases with higher body temperature and it’s useful in fighting virus load
  • Tests
    • Following tests to be done after 7 days if any discomfort / fever persists. If symptom free and oxygen saturation above 94 then measure your oxygen saturation after a six minute walk inside your room. If the saturation falls by 4 percent or more than still do the following tests. CBC, ESR, CRP, LFT, KFT, uric acid, Ferritin levels, D dimer, CK, CK-MB, Trop I, Pro BNP, Interleukin 6, HbA1c, HRCT thorax. If liver enzyme Alt ( alanine amino transferase ) ,ferritin ,haemoglobin are high and or there is myalgia ( deep muscle pain ) or you have been a cancer patient or suffer from a neuro degenerative disease I would be extra vigilant and search out more intensive medical resources including availability of oxygen and potentially access to a hospital.
  • Prescription Medication

    Keep these following medicines at home. Do not start eating any. Only eat if oxygen saturation starts dropping below 90 .. if you eat get to a hospital 1) stanozol 2 mg ( goes by the brand name .. neuro bal ( dosage is 3 times a day with full glass of water )

    If hospitalised or oxygen saturation falls then breathing exercises , lying on your stomach ( with pillows ) and humming all help. Remdesvir , plasma therapy etc have no efficacy ( I’ve read loads of clinical trial data )and am perplexed as to why they are prescribed other than the human instinct of trying something.

    Steroids ( usually dexmethazone and or methyl prednisone )work very well but do not self administer. If had to early they will suppress the fight against infections. Make sure you have these only under medical supervision.

    At that juncture doctors usually prescribe an anti coagulant like heparin etc. Do tell the doctor about your consumption of turmeric and garlic as they are both blood thinners and maybe the doctor might chose to modify dosage. The general perception is that in covid 19 and or covid 21 only the respiratory tract is involved but this is not true. That is where the symptoms are most acute.

    The reality is that the spike protein bonds with Ace2 receptors. These are embedded in virtually all membranes but have the highest density in the lungs , heart , blood vessels, brain ,gut,other organs ... hence it causes inflammation and different kinds of inflammations and sub optimal functioning in these organs which may or may not be symptomatic. Hence you hear of sudden cardiac arrests in patients whose lung functions etc have normalised. This dangerous effect can last for about three months after the acute symptomatic phase of the disease. In this period it is best not to stress your system. Do ambling walks , yoga , stretches etc . Let your system recover. Don’t attempt to do intense exercises.
